urban innovative temperature adjustment system

issue anchoring

This project addresses the growing risk of extreme urban heat driven by climate change. It proposes a distributed “solar umbrella” system that shifts heat management from passive mitigation to active, responsive control. Using grating structures and thermal materials, the system regulates solar radiation while integrating sensing and spatial monitoring to identify and respond to heat-intensive zones. By combining material-based cooling with real-time data feedback, it creates an adaptive urban system that improves thermal comfort and reduces heat-related risks.

what i try to do

Aim to create a system designed to help regulate temperatures within urban areas. By drawing upon principles inspired by NASA's deep-space communication technologies—such as radar sonification—I intend to construct a responsive system capable of real-time monitoring of urban cooling infrastructure and the effectiveness of its operations.

Assumption use flow

The system operates through a centralized platform that integrates environmental sensing, device deployment, and real-time monitoring for responsive urban cooling. Cooling is triggered either automatically based on environmental thresholds or manually by operators. The platform analyzes spatial data to identify heat-critical areas and deploys “solar umbrella” devices, which unfold to regulate solar radiation and reduce heat. Each device performs localized scanning, and the data is translated through an image–sound–image workflow for real-time visualization.

smart management system

The system uses a cross-modal workflow to convert spatial data into audio signals and reconstruct it into visual outputs. Environmental images are encoded into binary data and transformed into sound through sonification and signal modulation. The audio is transmitted and decoded by receivers, where it is reconstructed into images, similar to remote sensing and radar-based imaging processes. This continuous image–audio–image loop enables real-time visualization of temperature changes and cooling effects.

sky-ground information transfer

The system implements a cross-modal data translation workflow that converts spatial information into audio signals and reconstructs it into visual outputs. Environmental images captured via spatial scanning are encoded as binary data and transformed into sound via sonification and digital signal modulation techniques. These audio signals are transmitted to ground-based receivers, where they are decoded and reconstructed into images. This process is conceptually aligned with remote sensing and radar imaging systems, in which signals are translated into spatial representations, as well as with data transmission methods used in space communication. By continuously cycling through this image-audio-image pipeline, the system enables real-time visualization of temperature changes and cooling effects, allowing environmental dynamics to be monitored through both spatial and temporal patterns.

A functional prototype was developed to simulate device deployment and data transmission within the system. It uses a Raspberry Pi to coordinate signal input, mechanical actuation, and data processing, triggering the deployment of a servo-controlled panel. The prototype encodes binary data into visual (LED) and audio (buzzer) outputs, with GPS and audio modules supporting positioning and signal transmission. It validates the system logic by linking signal processing, physical response, and data feedback.
